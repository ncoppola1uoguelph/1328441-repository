During the requirements gathering stage, developers of a system use various methods to begin a rudimentary list of requirements. Methods can include interviewing clients or users, conducting surveys, observing interactions with their system and more. This part of the gathering is important because it lets the developers get an idea of their client’s needs. Afterwards, the developers assign requirements unique identification, and write their descriptions in simple language using the format: “The user/system must/should do one thing”. This is important because it makes the requirements easy to reference with their ID’s, and using the format in simple language makes each requirement easy for developers to understand and follow. Requirements are also assigned a priority and dependencies so the developers know which ones to work on first, and are organised in a table in a document. After completion, requirements are presented to inspectors for feedback. The requirements are made easily changeable because the process revises them several times. The process is important to the entire software development process because it helps developers understand the needs of their clients so they can make an efficient and intuitive system.
